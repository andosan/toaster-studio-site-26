01 / The view

Most projects fail not in the parts but in the spaces between them. A beautiful design that the team can't operate. A working system the audience won't trust. A clever tool nobody asked for. We work where the parts meet.

i.

The client.

The team that has to live with the result long after we leave. If the work doesn't fit how they actually operate, it dies on contact.

ii.

The audience.

The people the work is made for. Defense procurement officers, banking compliance teams, citizens reading a government portal — each reads the same artefact differently. The work has to land for them, not for us.

iii.

The work itself.

The artefact, the system, the interface. It has its own logic, constraints, and lifespan. We let it tell us what it wants to be, instead of forcing a template onto it.

iv.

The people building it.

Us, our partners, the tools we run. Three people, banking-grade engineering through Inetis, AI infrastructure where it belongs. The making is part of the system, not separate from it.
