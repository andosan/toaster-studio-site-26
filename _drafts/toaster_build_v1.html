Build.

A project. Defined scope, shipped result, end date.

i. What we build

Single deliverables with internal complexity.

Sites, systems, interfaces, identities. Most often for teams in defense, finance, governance, and selected commercial work. The work is usually a single deliverable with internal complexity — a portal that has to pass compliance review, a sales site that has to feel different from the rest of the category, a system that has to live alongside legacy infrastructure.

We don't take on every project. We take on projects where the four parts of the system — the client, the audience, the work, the people building it — have a chance of moving together. If one of those is broken before we start, the project will not survive contact with reality, and we'll say so.

ii. How a build works

Four phases. Plain language. No surprises.

Scope.

We write the scope together. Two to three working sessions, sometimes more for complex systems. The scope document is short, written in plain language, and includes what we will not do as clearly as what we will. You sign it; we sign it; it becomes the contract.

Make.

The studio delivers. Three people, with banking-grade engineering through Inetis when the work needs it — typically for compliance-heavy, security-sensitive, or large-scale systems. AI infrastructure is used where it earns its place: asset preparation, content production, internal tooling. It is not used where it doesn't belong — strategy, judgement, or anything where being wrong has consequences.

Ship.

We ship to staging early and often. You see the work growing, not a final reveal. When the work is ready for production, we hand over with documentation your team can read, not just your developers.

After.

We are available for a defined period after launch — typically four to eight weeks — for adjustments and the first round of real-world feedback. After that, the work is yours. If you want continued involvement, that is a different conversation: see adopt.

iii. Who delivers the work

Three people. Inetis when the work demands it.

Standard scope: the studio. Three people, working directly with you. No account managers, no junior staff learning on your project.

Banking-grade or large-scale scope: the studio plus Inetis. Inetis is a Swiss-Slovenian engineering partner with twenty years of work in regulated finance. They are named on the contract, named on this page, and visible from the first conversation. Subcontracting hidden behind our brand is not how we work.
