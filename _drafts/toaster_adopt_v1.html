Adopt.

A relationship. Your team learns to operate AI capability without us in the room.

i. What adopt is

The answer to a problem most teams already have.

AI is in the building, but nobody owns it. People are using ChatGPT in private. A junior is generating copy that nobody is editing. A senior is anxious that the tools are coming for the role. Some workflows have AI bolted on; others don't, for no good reason.

Adopt is not training. Training is a course you finish and forget. Adopt is a working relationship — three to six months, weekly cadence — where your team learns to operate AI inside the actual work they do, with someone senior in the room until they don't need one.

The goal is graduation, not retention. When your team can run the workflow without us, the engagement either ends or rotates to a new workflow. We measure success by your independence.

ii. Who adopt is for

Teams that produce serious work.

Content, design, code, research, analysis — and now have to decide what AI does and doesn't touch inside that work. Most often: marketing teams in regulated industries, in-house design and content teams, communications teams in institutions that cannot afford to get AI wrong in public.

Adopt is not for teams that want a tool recommendation. It is for teams that want a senior partner thinking alongside them about where AI earns its place in their work and where it doesn't belong at all.

iii. How an engagement works

Three phases over three to six months.

Total duration depends on the team's starting point and the depth of the workflows in scope.

Phase 01 Audit. Weeks 1—3

We map your current work. What you produce, how you produce it, where AI is already in use (often in private), where the obvious tedium lives, where AI should not be introduced. Output is a one-page roadmap, jointly owned, with priorities your team agrees with.

Phase 02 Pilot. Weeks 4—8

We rebuild one workflow with AI assistance, with two people from your team in the room throughout. The deliverable is not the rebuilt workflow — the deliverable is your team's confidence operating it without supervision. We pick a workflow that matters but isn't critical, so the cost of getting it wrong is low.

Phase 03 Embed. Weeks 9 →

Weekly office hours. Tool selection guidance. Edge case support. Light supervision as your team takes on more workflows on their own. The cadence drops as confidence rises. Engagement ends when your team operates independently — or rotates to the next workflow, if there is one.

iv. Who delivers

Founder-led. Every session.

Every conversation, every judgement call. There is no junior staff on adopt engagements, no rotation of consultants, no team behind the curtain. The work is delicate — when AI applies, when it doesn't, how to introduce it to a team without provoking the wrong reaction — and it is not something we delegate.

This is also why we hold the engagement count at two to three at a time. More than that, and the depth that makes adopt work disappears.
